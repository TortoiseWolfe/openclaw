## Key Concepts

A tweezer is a pattern used in technical analysis that helps show when a market might reverse direction. Traders can use the pattern on charts to detect tops and bottoms in prices so they can make better investment decisions. The pattern became widely accepted thanks to Steve Nison, an expert in candlestick analysis. Tweezer patterns can indicate bullish reversals, where prices may start rising, and bearish reversals, where prices may start falling. Tweezer patterns are reversal patterns and occur when two or more candlesticks touch the same bottom for a tweezer bottom pattern, or when two or more candlesticks touch the same top for a tweezer top pattern. Tweezer bottoms often signal short-term bullish reversals, while tweezer tops indicate bearish reversals. In both cases, buyers or sellers cannot push the price higher or lower. Both types of patterns require close observation and research to be interpreted and used correctly. A tweezers top is identified by two candles with similar highs occurring back to back. A tweezers bottom would see two candles instead with similar back-to-back lows. As part of an investment strategy, tweezers help traders pinpoint market trends. They appear in various forms, often at market turning points. Traders use these patterns to spot possible reversals or trade signals within the broader market context. Tweezers became mainstream in Steve Nison's book "Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques." Candlestick methods show a candle's body, formed by the open and close price, and thin "shadows" show the period's high and low. A dark or red candle means the close was lower than the open, while a white or green candle means the price closed higher. As with any other trading tool or indicator, tweezers should be used in conjunction with other indicators or market signals.
