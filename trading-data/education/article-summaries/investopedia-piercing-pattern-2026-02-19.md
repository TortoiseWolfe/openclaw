## Key Concepts

A piercing pattern is a two-day candlestick charting pattern that signals a potential short-term reversal from a downward trend to an upward trend. It includes the first day opening near the high and closing near the low with an average or larger-sized trading range, followed by a gap down on the second day, where trading opens near the low and closes near the high. The second day's close should cover at least half the upward length of the previous day's red candlestick body, indicating buyer enthusiasm and potential market support levels that traders may use alongside other technical indicators. The second candle of a piercing pattern should close above the midpoint of the preceding candle. This indicates that the buyers outnumbered the sellers for that trading period. The first candlestick is usually dark colored or red, signifying a down day, and the second is green or lighter colored, signifying a day that closes higher than it opened. When a trader is watching for a bullish reversal, any red candlestick followed by a white candlestick could be an alert, but the piercing pattern is a special indication because the reversal is likely unexpected for most market participants. In technical analysis, a piercing pattern can signal a potential bullish reversal . This pattern is rare, but it works better when preceded by a longer downtrend. If indicators like RSI, Stochastic, or MACD show bullish divergence along with a piercing pattern, it strengthens the signal. The second day’s white candlestick rebound from a down gap to a midpoint closing high is expected to be a sign that a support level has been reached. This may occur because the market specialist or market makers set the opening price lower than the previous day's close. A piercing pattern is typically only a potential signal for reversal so following a piercing pattern a trader would want to watch for a breakaway gap. A breakaway gap happens in the early stages of a reversal. It is identified by two consecutive white candlesticks with a second-day white candlestick that shows a substantial gap higher from the first day’s closing price to the second day’s opening price. A piercing pattern followed by a breakaway gap can be a strong affirmation that a reversal is occurring. In a bullish reversal, traders can buy the stock or purchase an in-the-money call option with a strike price below the market price. The bearish equivalent of a piercing pattern is a dark cloud pattern .
